Understanding Blood Pressure

Blood pressure is the force of blood pushing against your artery walls. It's measured with two values:

  • Systolic: Pressure during heartbeats.
  • Diastolic: Pressure between heartbeats.

Keeping your BP within the normal range reduces the risk of heart disease, stroke, and other serious health conditions.

Understanding Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how your body turns food into energy. When your blood sugar gets too high, it can damage organs and blood vessels over time.

Monitoring your glucose levels regularly helps prevent complications like kidney damage, nerve issues, and vision loss.

Your Blood Group

O+
🧬 Blood Group Types
  • O+: Universal Donor for positive blood types.
  • A+: Compatible with A+ and AB+ recipients.
  • B+: Compatible with B+ and AB+ recipients.
  • AB+: Universal Recipient (can receive any blood group).
  • O-: Universal Donor for all blood types (rare).
  • AB-: Rare blood type that can donate to AB+ and AB-.

Why Blood Group Matters

Knowing your blood group is important for medical situations like transfusions, surgeries, or organ donation. It helps ensure compatibility between donor and recipient.
